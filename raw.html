Format Description

This page gives detailed information for your route. It's primarily for our internal use, so it's rather cryptic. If you don't understand it, just ignore it. Warning: the format may change without notice.

Longitudes & latitudes are signed floating point values. Positive values are east & north. We always give longitude first. Distances are in kilometers, in floating point.

The ``Turn Information'' section has a series of lines. Each starts with a keyword. Here is a description of the interesting lines:

TURN # lng lat ptNdx #pts turnDeg outHdg inHdg km text-instructions
TurnDeg is the turn, in degrees (-179 to 180): 0 is straight, -90 is left, 90 is right, 180 is a u-turn, etc. OutHdg is the outgoing compass heading: 0 is north, 90 is east, 270 is west, etc. Someday inHdg will be the incoming heading, but for now it's -1. Km is the distance to the next turn. This segment has #pts shape points, starting with shape point number ptNdx.
START lng lat {place-id} text-description
There's one such line for the start, end, and each intermediate point. The place-id string is always in curly brackets. The place-id will not have blanks, but may be empty.
KM total-dist-in-km
MIN total-time-in-minutes
